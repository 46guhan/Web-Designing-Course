Did you know that honeybees communicate with each other through a "waggle dance" to share information about the location of food sources? The intricate dance, performed inside the hive, conveys both the direction and distance of a food source relative to the sun. The angle of the "waggle" part of the dance indicates the direction, while the duration of the waggle conveys the distance, allowing other bees to find the flowers and gather nectar.
